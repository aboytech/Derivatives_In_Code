## AAPL Log Returns — Interpretation

Based on *AAPL's* log returns, it has been **predominantly ranging from ±0.05** over the last 5 years. There are some rare scenarios in which it spiked over that range for a short period of time.

What is worth noticing is that, on **Q1 2025**, given *Trump's tariff announcements*, AAPL spiked over **-0.10 and almost 0.15**. This tells us that AAPL was significantly affected by these announcements given its manufacturing in China. However, this range can be interpreted as a *shock and a correction* right after that shock; first, AAPL got hit going down to -0.10, but in the days following, the market corrected after the initial selloff.

The gradient was near zero during the stable ±0.05 periods, confirming the returns weren't just small, they were **consistent**. The Q1 2025 gradient spike confirms the tariff shock was **abrupt, not a slow deterioration**, detectable as a regime shift that raw returns alone would not reveal.
